Oldest Temple In India: Mundeshwari Devi Temple

Mundeshwari devi temple is known as oldest temple in India; is located at Kaura in Kaimur district in the state of Bihar. It is located on the Mundeshwari Hill at an elevation of 608 feet (185 m) .

It is dedicated to the worship of Lord Shiva and Shakti. This temple has sculptures of other Gods and Goddesses as well which includes Lord Ganesha, Surya and Vishnu. Many say it to be the oldest temple in world to be functional till now.

As per the prevalent version, the temple was built in the period of 3-4 BC with the Narayana, or Vishnu, as the presiding deity. The statue of Narayana has disappeared due to the ravages of time. During 348 AD, a new deity Viniteswara was set up as a minor deity in the temple, holding a subsidiary position to Narayana, the main deity.

Around the seventh century AD, Shaivism became the prevalent religion and Viniteswara, which was a minor deity, emerged as the presiding deity of the temple. The Chatur Mukhalingam (Lingam with four faces) representing him was accorded the central place in the temple, which it holds even now.

After this period, the Cheros, a powerful aboriginal tribe and the original inhabitants of the Kaimur hills, ascended to power. The Cheros were worshippers of Shakti, as represented by Mundeswari, also known as Maheshmardini and Durga. Mundeswari was made the main deity of the temple. However, Mukhalingam still occupied centre stage in the temple. So the image of Durga was installed in a niche along one wall of the temple, where it resides to this day, while the Mukhalingam survives as the subsidiary deity, though in a central position.

The inscription of an information plaque erected by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) at the site indicates the dating of the temple to 108 AD. However, there are other versions for the dating stating the Saka era, prior to Gupta dynasty rule (320 AD) in India, and specifically to 105 AD according to the Administrator of the Bihar Religious Trust Board.

It is believed that rituals and worship have been performed here without a break, hence Mundeshwari is considered one of the most ancient Hindu temples in India. The temple is visited by a large number of pilgrims each year, particularly during the navami, Shivratri festivals. A big annual fair is held nearby during the Navratri visited by thousands.

The temple, built of stone, is on an octagonal plan which is rare. It is the earliest specimen of the Nagara style of temple architecture in Bihar. There are doors or windows on four sides and small niches for the reception of statues in the remaining four walls. The temple shikhara or tower has been destroyed. However, a roof has been built, as part of renovation work. The interior walls have niches and bold mouldings which are carved with vase and foliage designs. At the entrance to the temple, the door jambs are seen with carved images of Dvarapalas, Ganga, Yamuna and many other murtis. The main deities in the sanctum sanctorum of the temple are of the Devi Mundeshwari and Chaturmukh (four faced) Shiva linga. There are also two stone vessels of unusual design. Even though the Shiva linga is installed in the centre of the sanctum, the main presiding deity is Devi Mundeshwari deified inside a niche, which is seen with ten hands holding symbols riding a buffalo, attributed to Mahishasuramardini. The temple also has murtis of other popular gods such as Ganesha, Surya and Vishnu. A substantial part of this stone structure has been damaged, and many stone fragments are seen strewn around the temple. However, under the jurisdiction of ASI, it has been the subject of archaeological study for quite some time.

The plate at the site of the Temple gives a dates of 625 CE for the Temple. Hindu inscriptions dated 635 CE were found in the temple.

There are many Indian temples which were founded in prehistoric days and the subsequent rulers of the region have enhanced them. Hence, accurately deciding the antiquity of a temple is not very easy. Most of the ancient temples are dated based on the inscriptions found in them. However, the temple itself might be founded even centuries earlier. Based on the inscriptions and recorded history, here are some of the most ancient temples of India.

  • Sanchi Stupa - Buddhist religious place - 3rd century BC

The great Stupa of Sanchi is one of the oldest existing religious monument in India. It is located near Bhopal in Madhya Pradesh state. Sanchi Stupa was built by emperor Ashoka the great. It is a Buddhist place of worship. Architecture of Sanchi Stupa include two Dom shaped brick structures built on top of the relics of Shree Buddha. The construction of Sanchi Stupa happened during the 3rd century BC.

Photo: Via Flickr [CC BY-SA 2.0], via Wikimedia Commons

  • Buddhist cave temple - Ajanta cave 9 and 10 1st-2nd century BC

Cave 10 in Ajanta is one of the oldest in the site. It belong to the last centuries of BC.This cave has an array of simple octagonal pillars and a Stupa in the middle of the central hall. This is a Chaitya Gruha or Buddhist prayer hall belong to the Hinayana sect of Buddhism

Photo: By Anahgem (Own work) [CC BY-SA 3.0], via Wikimedia Commons

  • Cave temples of Badami - around AD 578

Chalukyas were a powerful dynasty that ruled most parts of North Karnataka from the year AD 544. There are 4 cave temples in Badami, built around 540600AD. Accurate dating is only done for the 3rd cave. An inscription found inside this temple attributing its construction to Mangalesha- the third king of Chalukya dynasty. The year and day of consecration of this temple is also clearly marked. It is equivalent to the full moon day of 1st November in AD 578. In fact, the date was written in Saka year.

  • Lad Khan temple, Aihole - AD 450

Lad Khan temple is one of the ancient temples in South India. The original name of this temple is not known. Currently it known after the name of a former occupant of the temple named Lad Khan. He was a Muslim saint who occupied the abandoned temple centuries after its construction. Even though there are no clear archaeological evidences, this temple is believed to be built in AD 450.

Image: Pick, Pack, Go

  • Durga temple, Aihole - AD 748

This temple was built in AD 748 and was dedicated to God Surya (the sun God). Later, with the changing ideologies of Hinduism, the temple was re-dedicated to God Vishnu. There was a small watchtower on top this temple locally known as Durg (or Fort). The locals called this temple The Durg temple or later it was known as Durga temple. The shape of Durga temple is very intriguing. It resembles the structure of Indian parliament house.

Image: Pick, Pack, Go

  • Virupaksha temple. Pattadakkal - AD 745

This is an ancient temple built by Queen of Chalukyan king Vikramaditya II in 745. Originally the deity was known as Lokeshwara named after the chief patron of this temple Queen Lokamahadevi . According to the inscription on the octagonal column at the center of the Pattadakkal site, Virupaksha temple was built to commemorate the victory of Vikramaditya II against the Pallavas and conquering Kancheepuram in 745.

  • Virupaksha temple, Hampi - AD 1200 (modified 1500)

Photo By Apadegal [CC BY-SA 3.0], via Wikimedia Commons

Virupaksha temple is one of the most visited temples in Hampi. It was built by the famous Vijayanagara rule Krishna DevaRaya around 1500. However, there are evidences that Virupaksha temple existed much before this. An ancient slab tablet dated 1199 can be found near the entrance of this shrine. It describes about the Pampa- Virupaksha worship, which is the earliest record of the same. From this inscription, it becomes evident that the religious cult of worshipping the Virupaksha-PampaDevi couple existed much before the Vijayanagara period. The Vijayanagara rules must have just followed the tradition of the region.

  • Cave temples of Mahabalipuram

History of Mahabalipuram from 6th century AD is very well documented. Majority of the sculptures and temples in Mahabalipuram is attributed to the great Pallava dynasty starting with Simhavishnu. From 550AD, Pallavas created the cave temples (Mantapas), Monolithic temples (Rathas) and structural temples (like Shore temple) in Mahabalipuram.

  • The Kailaa temple - 756-773 AD

The Kailasnath Temple in Ellora it's one of the largest ancient temples in India. It was built by Rashtrkuta King Krishna 1 and is dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Concealing of PVC conduits-

Lay out and mark the path of your electrical conduit and electrical boxes as specified in the blueprints.

Work shall be commenced after fixing of steel reinforcement on centering material.

Conduits shall be firmly fixed on steel of RCC work by binding wire. This shall be done for every 4' along the conduit and try to tie it at the point the horizontal and vertical bars cross. Fixing of conduits shall be such that it will remain rigid during casting of slab, beam, and column even after use of vibrator.

Make sure that you leave 12" of the conduit outside of the form so you can join it later to the rest of the buildings electrical supply.

Deep junction boxes and other draw-in boxes shall be such that their open end and cantering material will not have gap in between so as to avoid concrete entering inside even after fixing covers to steel reinforcement; and be filled with dry sand.

Open ends of conduits; to be concealed in walls, shall be provided with couplers / sockets at ends and be flush with bottom of beam, and located at the center of the beam.

As far as possible bunching / grouping of conduits shall be avoided so that it will not affect strength of RCC work especially in beams.

Suitable steel fish wire shall be drawn through in the conduits for drawing of wires later on.

Shilaaidevi temple discovered nr TRIMBAK

In 1964 by Khanderao Navalkar and wife Shantabai in the Jungles of Beje , a tribal area near the famous Trimbakeshwar Temple. They can be seen placing the Kalash or crown on its dome.
