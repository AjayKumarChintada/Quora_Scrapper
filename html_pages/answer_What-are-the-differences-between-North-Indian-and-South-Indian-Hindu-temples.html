There are many differences between North Indian and South Indian Hindu temples, including their architecture, rituals, and deities worshipped. Here are a few examples:

  1. Architecture: One of the most noticeable differences between North Indian and South Indian temples is their architecture. North Indian temples tend to have a more ornate and elaborate style, with tall spires and intricate carvings, while South Indian temples tend to have a simpler and more functional design, with a focus on large open spaces.
  2. Rituals: The rituals and ceremonies performed in North Indian and South Indian temples can also vary significantly. For example, in North Indian temples, puja (worship) is typically performed by a priest, while in South Indian temples, it is more common for worshippers to participate in the rituals themselves.
  3. Deities worshipped: The deities worshipped in North Indian and South Indian temples can also differ. In North Indian temples, it is common to find images of gods and goddesses from the Vedas, such as Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva, while in South Indian temples, the focus is often on local deities and village gods.

It is worth noting that these are just a few examples of the differences between North Indian and South Indian temples, and there is a great deal of diversity within each region as well. There are many factors that contribute to the differences between temples, including regional customs, cultural traditions, and historical influences.
