In Istanbul the Hagia Sophia was the greatest Church in the Greco-Roman Christian world. It was converted into a mosque, then a museum and now it is back being a mosque once again. But it is still a Church, built as a Church by Christians and functioned as a Church for centuries.

Angkor Wat, temple complex was built in the 12th century by King Surya varman II (reigned 1113c. 1150). It was built by a Hindu king as a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, and is covered with hundreds of sculptures from Hindu mythology. It was dedicated and consecrated by Brahmins with ancient Hindu rituals and functioned as a Hindu temple for centuries.

The central deity - Vishnu - was evicted and Buddha installed - everything else has remained exactly as it was before. The vast religious complex of Angkor Wat comprises more than a thousand buildings, and it is one of the great cultural wonders of the world. So regardless of who is now in control it was, is and always will be a Hindu temple complex and it is still the largest religious complex on the planet.

If you do a poll of Hindu in India, 90% of them will not really understand Vishnu other than as a paramatma or as the avatara Rama and Krishna. Hence the need for bhakti traditions.

What Maharshi Gautama tried to do is to find a way to put people on the path without needing these spiritually abstract concepts, which is what all bhakti traditions do by layering folklore over knowledge. The knowledge part in bhakti traditions are disseminated using models of the spiritual realm - temples - and by many other means such as the satsang, paath, gurbani, nritya/natya, bhajan and so on, which people who are older and more experienced utilize. But there is a gap in the offerings, knowledge to those who are not so intellectually inclined as to be committed to jnanayog for a lifetime, one that Bauddha fulfills successfully.

The fact that the locals call it a Buddhist temple (assuming they do) is really nice. It means that for the locals, there is no difference between Bauddha and other Hindu traditions. They are all part of one universe!
