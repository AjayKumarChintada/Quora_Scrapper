Yes, there was a Samaritan temple on Mt Gerizim. The site was excavated starting in the early 1980’s and the temple dates probably to the 5th century BCE. The story according to Josephus is that it was destroyed sometime in the second century BCE and rebuilt, only to be destroyed again by Judean forces on orders of the Maccabean Yohanon Cohen Gadol, the Judean High Priest, before the beginning of the first century BCE. There was religious rivalry between Judeans and Samaritans, since Samaritans claimed Mt Gerizim was the site chosen by God for the binding of Isaac, rather than Mt Moriah in Jerusalem. Also, the situation in those times was quite unsettled for independent Judea owing to incursions by the Seleucid Empire.
