CORRECT ANSWER In Sanskrit, vapi means a well, and gyan means the knowledge that liberates. Thus, gyanvapi refers to a well of knowledge.

It was created at a time when even the holy Ganges had not descended to earth. Lord Shiva dug this well with his trishul to get water for his puja. He then delivered a spiritual sermon to his consort Parvati. Thus, the well came to be known as gyanvapi.

Hence, Gyanvapi belongs to only and only Lord Shiva.
